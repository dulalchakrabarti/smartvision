A Smart-vision Tool Empowering Gen-Z Farmers
Field level monitoring of crop health is crucial for precision agriculture,
crop insurance, and climate risk management. This study focuses on the ability 
of Google Gemini, a multi modal generative AI chat bot, to monitor crop health
and estimate wheat yield using sequences of geotagged smartphone images as 
actionable evidence. A total of 118 field observations covering nine wheat varieties
from Haryana and Punjab (2017–18 Rabi season) were processed and analyzed. 
Gemini-based visual estimates ranged from 14.5 to 27.2 quintals per acre 
(mean 21.8 ± 2.1 q/acre) and were in quite good agreement with actual 
values evaluated using Crop Cutting Experiment (CCE). Lodging was identified 
as the dominant yield-limiting factor, causing yield penalties of 0–36% 
depending on severity and timing. A practical yield adjustment framework 
using Growing Degree Days (GDD) was developed and validated on 30 independent 
observations (Mean Absolute Error = 1.2 q/acre, Root Mean Square Error = 1.6 q/acre)
, with 85% of predictions falling within ±1.5 q/acre of observed values. 
The results demonstrate that multi modal generative AI can effectively transform 
farmer-captured image sequences into reliable phenological, stress, and yield insights. 
This approach offers a scalable, low-cost solution for digital agriculture in 
resource-constrained smallholder systems.
Keywords: Generative AI, Crop yield , Digital agriculture, Smart-vision, Crop Dignostics
